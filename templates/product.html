Comply with regulations

In Alberta, the Power Engineers Regulation requires that certain power plants, thermal liquid heating systems, and heating plants update and maintain a log book. This log book can be electronic, if it has certain features.

TidyLog is an electronic log book that meets all regulatory requirements:

Permanent record

Prevents revisions, additions, or deletions to data which has been previously recorded.

Review and acknowledge

Allows operators and engineers to review the log entries and acknowledge entries by shift. Tracks reviews and acknowledgements.

Allows a safety codes officer to review the log.

Allows an engineer to comment on the log without modifying the original data.

Secure

Unique and secure accounts identifies the employee entering, reviewing, and acknowledging logs.

Tracked

Each entry, review, and acknowledgement in the log is time-stamped with the date and time of the event.

Each entry, review, and acknowledgment tracks the employee who performed the activity in the log.
